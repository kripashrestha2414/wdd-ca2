Practical Ways to Reduce Plastic

Where Does Plastic Show Up?

Plastic sneaks into many areas of our lives. May it be your food packaging, single-use bags, bottles, wraps, and even cosmetics. By paying attention to where you commonly encounter plastic, you can make focused swaps that make a big environmental impact.

Easy Plastic Alternatives

  1. Bring Your Own Bags
    Use reusable shopping bags (cotton or canvas) instead of accepting plastic or paper bags at shops. Keep one in your bag or car to avoid forgetting it at checkout till.
  2. Skip Plastic Produce and Bulk Bags
    Rather than using free plastic bags at farmerâ€™s markets or bulk shelves, bring your own mesh or cloth produce bags or reuse glass jars. Many stores allow you to weigh your container beforehand and subtract it at checkout.
  3. Ditch Plastic Wrap
    Replace cling film or plastic bags with reusable alternatives like beeswax wraps, glass containers, or platinum silicone storage. These options can be washed and reused for years, avoiding single-use plastic entirely.
  4. Bring Your Own Utensils & Straws
    Carry your own reusable straws (bamboo, metal, or glass) and travel utensil set to avoid disposable versions when eating out or ordering takeout.
  5. Use a Travel Mug
    Refusing single-use cups by bringing a travel mug can reduce plastic and paper waste. Many coffee shops even offer discounts if you bring your own cup.
